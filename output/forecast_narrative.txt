PIPELINE DEMAND FORECAST — 30/60 DAY PROJECTION
==================================================

METHOD
Each forecasted month is projected as the trailing 3-month average of actual throughput. This is a moving-average forecast — it assumes the near-term future will resemble the recent past, and does not model trend, seasonality, or known planned events.

FORECAST
  April 2026: 97.21
  May 2026: 97.21

ASSUMPTIONS
- Assumes no unplanned outages, apportionment events, or maintenance
  windows beyond the pattern already reflected in the trailing average.
- Assumes no material change in available capacity in the forecast window.
- Does not account for seasonality; the dataset does not show a strong
  consistent seasonal pattern, so none was modeled.

RECENT VARIANCE CONTEXT (trailing 12 months of actuals)
  Mean: 92.88
  Std deviation: 7.31
  Range: 70.86 to 99.44

This range matters more than the single-point forecast above: the historical
data shows the system can drop sharply (see Section 5.3 of the main analysis
write-up) within a single month due to maintenance or apportionment events,
recovering within 1-2 months. A single-point moving-average forecast will NOT
predict when such an event occurs — it only reflects the recent steady-state.
A realistic forecast range should widen by roughly one standard deviation
in either direction to reflect this known volatility.
